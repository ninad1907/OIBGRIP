Vinayak . D . Savarkar

1883 - 1966

Father of Hindutva

Vinayak Damodar Savarkar also known as Veer Savarkar , was an Indian politician, activist, and writer . Vinayak Damodar Savarkar was born on 28 May 1883 in the Marathi Chitpavan Brahmin Hindu family in the village of Bhagur, near the city of Nashik, Maharashtra . Savarkar was greatly influenced by the radical Nationalist leader, Lokmanya Tilak . Savarkar began his political activities as a high school student and continued to do so at Fergusson College in Pune . He and his brother founded a secret society called Abhinav Bharat Society. Abhinav Bharat's main objectives were to overthrow British rule and reviving Hindu pride . Savarkar endorsed the idea of India as a Hindu Rashtra (Hindu Nation) . Savarkar was sentenced to life terms of imprisonment totaling fifty years and was moved to the Cellular Jail in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands . He wrote 38 books in English and Marathi , consisting in many essays, two novels called Moplah Rebellion and the Transportation, poetry and plays, the best-known of his books being his historical study The Indian war of independence, 1857 and his pamphlet Hindutva: Who Is a Hindu?.
